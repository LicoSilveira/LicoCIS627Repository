# Petco Park Analytics Innovation Project

**Project Title:** Quantifying the Causal Impact of Petco Park on San Diego Padres Attendance and Revenue

**Author:** Licurgo Silveira Teixeira  
**Course:** CIS627 – Sports Analytics  
**Date:** April 2026

## Project Overview
This project aims to measure the long-term causal effect of the 2004 relocation to Petco Park on fan attendance, ticket revenue, merchandise sales, and overall fan engagement for the San Diego Padres — a small-market MLB team competing in a city with strong outdoor lifestyle and competing entertainment options.

## Decision-Making Problem
Small-market teams like the Padres struggle to compete financially with large-market clubs. The move to Petco Park was a major strategic investment. Leadership needs to understand:  
- How much of the attendance and revenue growth was truly caused by the new downtown stadium?  
- How does the stadium interact with team performance and city lifestyle factors?  
- Should the organization invest further in stadium experience and fan engagement initiatives?

## Proposed Analytics Approach
The project uses historical data (2000–2025) including attendance, revenue, team performance (wins, payroll), and external factors. Regression analysis with proper controls will be applied to isolate the stadium’s causal impact from confounding variables such as team success and economic conditions.

## Use by Decision Makers
- **General Manager & Baseball Operations**: Evaluate long-term ROI of stadium investments.  
- **VP of Revenue & Marketing**: Optimize ticket pricing, fan engagement campaigns, and merchandise strategy.  
- **Fan Experience team**: Design better downtown activation and game-day experience.
